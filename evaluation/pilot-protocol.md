# SignBridge Pilot Protocol

Status: draft pending Deaf educator review. Do not begin with minors until the educator approves the language, consent process, and assessment items.

## Purpose

Evaluate whether one SignBridge session improves independent use of a targeted written-English grammar pattern for ASL-using learners ages 12–18.

## Participants And Consent

- Recruit 3–8 learners through the participating educator.
- Obtain educator/institution approval and parent or guardian consent where required.
- Obtain learner assent in accessible language; participation is voluntary and can stop at any time.
- Record no names, video, contact details, disability records, or school identifiers in SignBridge.
- Assign anonymous participant IDs such as `P01`.

## Procedure

1. Explain that the tool evaluates writing, not intelligence or ASL ability.
2. Administer the 10-item pre-assessment without corrections or hints.
3. Let the learner complete one or two SignBridge grammar sessions.
4. Administer the equivalent 10-item post-assessment without corrections or hints.
5. Ask confidence from 1–5 and three short usability questions.
6. Have the Deaf educator review diagnosis accuracy, cultural respect, meaning preservation, and explanation clarity.

### Visual Concept Bridge Observation

For at least three learner-authored sentences, record whether the role cards:

- preserved the learner's intended meaning;
- represented the learner's thought structure respectfully without implying one universal ASL order;
- made the written-English reordering or grammar-marker change easier to notice;
- could be rebuilt without adult assistance;
- led to an appropriate next action from the agent.

Ask the learner to explain one transformation in their own words. Record the explanation verbatim only with consent; otherwise record `understood`, `partly understood`, or `not understood`.

Ask the Deaf educator/tester to mark each concept-role assignment as `accurate`, `acceptable`, or `misleading`. Any `misleading` result blocks outcome claims until the prompt, fallback, or interface is corrected.

## Measures

- Independent pre/post accuracy using the fixed rubric.
- Guided attempts, hint levels, and unseen-transfer success from the local profile export.
- Completion time and learner confidence.
- Qualitative feedback: understandable, respectful, and useful.
- Concept-role accuracy and meaning-preservation rating.
- Visual-bridge completion and number of card moves.
- Whether the selected adaptive route was instructionally appropriate.
- Whether each qualitative memory statement matched actual prior evidence.

## Reporting Rules

- Report sample size and method beside every result.
- Present individual and aggregate results without identifying learners.
- Separate technical benchmark accuracy from learner outcome evidence.
- Report negative findings and limitations; never infer population-wide impact from a small pilot.
